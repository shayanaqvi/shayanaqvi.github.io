A Breath of Fresh Air

I use an old iMac. It's from 2012, and (obviously) came with macOS. The last version of macOS this thing got was Catalina. It ran like trash. Literally trash. I couldn't even right-click in peace, without the damn thing freezing.

My brother recommended I give Linux a try, and I did. What a change it made! A breath of fresh air! I could actually use the computer again.

Once that honeymoon period wore off, I found that plenty of distributions didn't run all that well. Too much cruft. It was usable, mind you, but still quite sluggish.

Now, I know close to nothing about how Void Linux works. All I can say about it is that it is fast. The fastest operating system I have put on this computer. Another breath of fresh air!

Heck, I could even run KDE Plasma on Void at a reasonable speed. It wasn't great, but it was miles better than Kubuntu, or any other distribution with Plasma.

Void Linux with a window manager, or even just XFCE, ran so much better than Plasma. I still find it a bit sluggish at times, but I tell you, it is not comparable to what macOS ran like.

Slackware, at least when it came to running the OS, was also pretty fast. That didn't stick with me because of the situation with installing software. It's fine, it works, but it's slow. Hence, back to Void.
