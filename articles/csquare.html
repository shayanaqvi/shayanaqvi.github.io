A Different Way of Completing the Square

I have not used this method enough to say for certain if this has any speed benefits. I did find it interesting nonetheless.

We're familiar with how we solve a quadratic through completing the square the standard way. Take \(x^2+8x-7\):

The other way is to solve them algebraically. Take a look at the photo below:

Essentially, we create an equation where our original quadratic equals our desired final form. Then, we bring the final form into quadratic form, and compare both sides of the equation.

All is fine and dandy till this point. My problem with this method is this: how do we know our final form? It could be \((ax+b)^2-r\), or it could be \(a(x+b)^2-r\). Others may exist.

On these grounds, I think the original method is superior. It's fast, and it works (as long as you know what you're doing).

Edit 08/09/2023: this seems obvious in hindsight, but this method works if you know the final form of the equation.
