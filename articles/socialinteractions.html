Social Interactions in a World of Smartphones

No, I am not the first to notice this. Nor will I be the last. In my eyes, this is a very real problem.

I find, that in today's world, it can be hard to interact with some people because of phones. Not all people, mind you, there are plenty who don't seem to have this problem. But there are some who do. For some people, you simply cannot interact with them without the phone being present in some way. Even if they don't use it, it's in their hands, always ready to be accessed.

I suppose a portion of the problem concerns the usage of the phone rather than the phone itself. But the usage of the phone tends to come from a certain kind of upbringing. This upbringing is one where the phone is the way to handle the child when they are very young. The phone is the way to keep them busy when they are a bit older. The phone what you give them when they are 10 years old. The phone is what you give them unrestricted access to. I'm sure it's not hard to see the problem here.

So when a child grows up with a phone constantly in their face, it's inevitable that they continue this habit when they get older.

The phone is the solution to boredom, to social awkwardness, to other similar inconveniences. The instant gratification of being momentarily entertained and distracted from the situation at hand must be a very strong and attractive force.

The phone is growing to become (or has become) a very big problem, at least in my eyes. It's at the point where its benefits are beginning to subdue to its ever-increasing drawbacks.
