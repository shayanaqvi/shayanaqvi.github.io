Why Tech Literacy is Important

I'll leave the definition and extent of tech literacy up to you to decide.

But in essence, it's important to be technologically literate so that corporations can't make a fool of you. Google, as an example, would love to DRM the web under the guise of protecting the user, and people who don't know any better believe this stuff. They believe that Google cares about them, and thus support these kinds of monopolistic efforts that benefit nobody but Google.

By choosing to not be reasonably literate about the devices/software we use a lot nowadays, we're allowing ourselves to be made a fool of. We're allowing companies to profit off of us like never before. We're allowing companies to abuse our willingness to use their products.

We're keeping corporations in power. We don't want that, do we?
