The Compromise I

It's pretty common knowledge that in order to avail a degree of privacy on the internet you've got to compromise convenience or ease-of-use.

Search engines suck. All of them. The ones that give you good good search results are privacy nightmares (kind of (but not very) logically). The ones that actually kind of care about your privacy give you really bad results.

I'm trying out Mojeek. By golly, unless I search for something or someone really well known, I find that close to all results are terrible. I tend to simply copy my search query, and search for the same thing in Duckduckgo.

Duckduckgo ain't great. But you know what? I feel like its the best middle ground there is. Let me clarify, that this is not a high bar to clear. Duckduckgo (most probably) does not care about you or your privacy. But it's better than Google in that regard and gives somewhat decent search results.

Apparently Brave isn't that much better, and they seem like kind of a shady company with some things they've done. Their results, from what I recall, in my experience, were worse than Duckduckgo, but most definately better than Mojeek.

I suppose there are two problems (with search engines). There's getting away from Google/Microsoft & company, then there's protecting your privacy. If you choose to ignore your privacy, you'll get a moderately good experience (sans privacy). If you choose to forgo Google/Microsoft, you'll also get a moderately okay experience. But if you choose to protect your privacy AND get away from big tech, you're in for a hard time right now.

Such is the situation. Isn't it a pity?
